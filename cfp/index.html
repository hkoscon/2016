Call for Proposals Results

CFP Results are Here!

We are pleased to announce the CFP results. We have received 48 submissions before our deadline 2016/02/28 23:00 (GMT+8). The volunteers in our Program Committee had a hard time to shortlist them to fit in our agenda.

Here is the list of the accepted CFP topics:

  • Free and Open Source Software Involvement in Taiwan / 回顧台灣自由開源軟體發展 (Jim Huang)
  • Interactive Dashboard Development with Plotly (Mart van de Ven)
  • Fluentd: Unified Logging Layer (Masahiro Nakagawa)
  • Offline first and search on steroids with Couchbase Mobile and Elasticsearch (Vegard Haugstvedt)
  • Containers, VMs, Processes… Isolation, performances, I/O… How all of these technologies work and compare to each other? Deep dive and learn about your Operating System (Quentin Adam)
  • A DevOps State of Mind (Chris Van Tuin)
  • Optimizing Chinese Webfont & Typography (Andy Shu)
  • 10 Reasons Why You Should Start Analytics Project with PostgreSQL (Satoshi Nagayasu)
  • From Open Source to Open Media (Hsin-Chan Chien)
  • How to build a NewSQL database? (Qi Liu)
  • Connecting Internet of Things to the Cloud with MQTT (Leon Anavi)
  • Developing applications with React and Redux (Anish Patil)
  • Migrating ODF and LibreOffice in Taiwan: Government, Enterprise and NPO (Franklin Weng)
  • Pentest Fun with Metasploit (Hayden Chung)
  • Fuzzing and Mozilla: 2015 (Gary Kwong)
  • How to Secure Your MySQL Database (Ivan Tu)
  • Understanding JS (Daniel Chcouri)
  • My Mozilla Contributing Journey (Irvin Chen)
  • Be Innovative with Copyright - Introduction to Open Source Business Models (Haggen So)
  • Fun with Router on Openwrt (Chris Choy)
  • Mozilla Eir - Bring medical devices into the world of web (Kevin Hu)
  • YAJS.vim and Vim Syntax System (Wei-Ko Kao)
  • What is FreeBSD and how to become part of the project (Marcelo Araujo)
  • Next Generation Config Mgmt (James Shubin)
  • apt-get install <my_software>: Packaging for Linux (Andy Li)
  • Creating your MySQL Group Replication Environment (Ivan Ma)
  • Practical computing for everyone (Chung-hong Chan)

If your topic is not selected, don't worry. You may still share your topic in our Unconference Session at Lunch of Day 2 (Saturday).
